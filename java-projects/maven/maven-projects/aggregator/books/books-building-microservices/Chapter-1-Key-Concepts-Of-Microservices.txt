What are Microservices?
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Microservices can change, deployed and released independently.
Microservices are designed around a business domain.
Microservices are opinionated, they are designed to do one thing well.
Microservice Information Hiding, they hide their implementation details and only expose a well-defined API.
Loosely coupled systems with autonomous teams.
Building system that can change to meet need of user.

Previously Teams were divided based on their Core technical competencies (Frontend, Backend, Database, etc.) but with microservices,
teams are organized around business capabilities or domains.
Each team is responsible for the entire lifecycle of their microservice, from development to deployment and maintenance.
This promotes ownership and accountability, allowing teams to work more autonomously and make decisions that best serve their specific domain.

Advantages of Microservices:
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Technology Heterogeneity
Robustness: Bulkhead (If one system fail it should not cascade across the system)
Scaling: Scale only the services that need it, rather than the entire application. (On demand provisioning: AWS)
Ease of Deployment, Independent Deployability
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Limitations of Microservices:
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cannot run more applications locally.
Data Consistency (Multiple db per service)
Latency
Service Modeling
Cost
Reporting and Analytics
Monitoring and Debugging
Security
Testing
